Savory Home Style Pot Roast

Description:

It's a classic that we all know and love. Tender beef in a succulent gravy with onions, carrots, and potatoes. Serve it with whatever sides you like but make sure to share it with friends and family. And tomorrow, you can make an open faced sandwich with the leftovers! ðŸ¤¤
It takes a little time but is otherwise super simple to make. Sear the meat, soften the onions, add aromatics, add the meat back to the pot, let it sit in the oven a while, add the potatoes and carrot then eat.

Ingredients

  • Large yellow onion
  • Garlic (3+ cloves)
  • Roast
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Thyme
  • Sage
  • Carrots
  • Red potatoes
  • Bay leaves
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Beef broth/stock
  • Flour (2 tblespn)

Let's Cook It!

  1. Season the meat generously with salt and pepper. Ideally let it sit over night or even
    longer to soak up the seasoning. Sear the meat on all sides in the Dutch oven on high heat
    (about 6 minutes a side)
  2. Dice a larger yellow onion (or 2 little ones) and soften in the same Dutch oven at medium-low heat.
    When nearly translucent add the thyme and sage to give an extra herb-y goodness to the gravy.
  3. Once the onions are translucent, clear a space in the middle of the pot to add the garlic (either
    finely chopped or use a garlic press) and let it get fragrant (about a minute) then mix in. Sprinkle
    2 tblspns of flower and mix in then slowly add beef stock stirring the whole time
  4. Put the beef in the pot, add more beef stock if necessary, add a couple bay leaves and a dash of
    Worcestershire. Cover and let braise in a 300Â° oven for ~3.5 hour. Add whatever veggies you like
    for the last hour and a half (potatoes, carrots, celery, etc.)
  5. Pull it out of the oven when itâ€™s as tender as you want it (I like it to be not quite fall apart fork tender yet),
    hit the meat with some fresh cracked pepper, and let it rest for about 10-15 minutes.

Beef and Cheese Enchiladas

Description:

Theese beefy and cheesy enchiladas will have your mouth watering and your home smelling like heaven. A little bit sweet, a little bit spicy and very very cheesy, this versatile meal can be filled with any meat you choose. In this case, we're going with ground beef but it could be chicken, carne adovada, or even vegetarian. The world's your oyster, babe.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (or brisket)
  • 1/2 onion
  • Tortillas
  • Enchilada sauce
  • Chipotle sauce (optional)
  • Cilantro
  • Cheese
  • 1/2 tbsp Cumin
  • Garlic
  • 1/2 tbsp Chili powder
  • Green chilies if you want (I don’t like them)
  • 2 Tbsp corn starch

Let's Cook It!

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 350°
  2. Dice the onions and sweat them with the meat as it browns.
  3. When the mat is browned and the onions are translucent, add garlic.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, cumin, and chili powder (about a half a table spoon each).
  5. (Optional) Add some chipotle sauce if you want it (you do)
  6. Heat the enchilada sauce and thicken with a corn starch slurry if necessary. (I really like to use a
    Red Chile or Chile Colorado sauce here).
  7. Put a little sauce in the bottom of the baking dish. Fill tortillas with sauce meat and cheese,
    roll and place seam side down in dish. When finished, drizzle some more sauce on top, any extra
    meat and some cheese.
  8. Bake at 350°F until brown and bubbly good (~25-35 minutes).
  9. Chop up cilantro and put it on top
  10. LET IT REST. It's hot and I don't want you burning your tongue
  11. Serve it up. I like to have mine with refired beans and brown rice
    (beacue brown rice tastes so much better)

Cozy Chicken n' Dumplings

Description:

The perfect companion to a rainy day. This chicken n dumplings recipe will warm your bones and bring your soul alive.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Onion - 1/2 or 1 small
  • Carrots - match diced onion volume
  • Celery - match diced onion volume
  • Garlic - 2 cloves
  • Thyme
  • Flour
  • Baking powder
  • Chicken broth - 4 cups
  • Chicken
    • pre-cooked and shredded
    • Or raw thighs, 1.5-2 lbs should do it
  • Milk - 1/2 cup

Let's Cook It!

Soup:

  1. Dice up onions, carrots and celery (probably half an onion for a smaller batch and a whole
    onion for more people). Just have about the same amount of each.
  2. Put a bit of oil in the pot and some butter (optional) then sweat the veggies.
  3. Make a little well in the middle of the pot and squeeze in 2 cloves of garlic (or mince
    the garlic, grate it on a micro plane. Whatever). Let it get fragrant (about a minute)
    then mix it in.
  4. Add about a teaspoon of dried thyme and mix in - I do more like a tablespoon and a half.
  5. When the veggies have softened add a few heaping tablespoons of flour (you can go as far as
    1/4 cup or 4tbsp). Mix it in so the flour soaks up all the moisture.
  6. Add the chicken broth, I added an extra cup because I used more veggies.
  7. Chicken - either used pre-cooked chicken, shred and dump it in now. Or add boneless skinless
    chicken thighs to the boiling broth and cook until almost all the way done
    (I think it’s about 25-30ish minutes). Then take ‘em out, shred ‘em, and add ‘em back to the soup.
    Reduce heat to a simmer.
  8. Season to taste (it won’t need much)

Dumplings

  1. In a separate bowl, Combine 3/4 cup of flour, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, a teaspoon-ish of thyme,
    pinch of salt and pepper and mix.
  2. Mix in 1/2 cup of milk, don’t over mix. Just enough to absorb the milk into the flour.
    Go for a little thicker than pancake batter
  3. Use 2 spoons to drop spoonfuls of the dumpling batter into the simmering soup. I find using a smaller
    spoon is best because the dumplings puff up as they steam. Put the lid on the pot and let steam for 10 minutes
